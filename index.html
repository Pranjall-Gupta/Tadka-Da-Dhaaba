Gulab Jamun


Ingredients


For the Jamun (dumplings):

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp milk (adjust as needed)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda (optional)
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • 1 tsp rose water or a few strands of saffron (optional)

Recipe


  1. Prepare sugar syrup: Boil sugar and water until sugar dissolves. Add crushed cardamom and rose water. Keep warm.

  2. Prepare dough: Crumble khoya into a bowl. Mix maida and baking soda. Add a little milk to form a smooth, soft dough.

  3. Fry Jamuns: Heat ghee on medium-low flame. Fry balls until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towel.

  4. Soak in syrup: Immediately immerse fried balls in warm sugar syrup for 2-3 hours before serving.

Ras Malai


Ingredients


For the Ras Malai balls:

  • 1 liter milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida) or cornflour
  • 4-5 cardamom pods (crushed)

For the Rabri (milk syrup):

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron
  • 2 tsp chopped pistachios or almonds (for garnish)

Recipe


  1. Prepare Chhena(paneer): Boil milk and add lemon juice to curdle. Drain curds in muslin cloth, rinse to remove sourness, squeeze out excess water.

  2. Make balls: Knead chhena until smooth. Mix sugar and flour. Shape into small flat discs.

  3. Boil Ras (syrup): Boil water with sugar and cardamom. Add chhena discs, cook on medium flame for 15-20 minutes. Cool.

  4. Prepare Rabri: Boil milk slowly, reduce to half. Add sugar, cardamom, saffron. Let cool.

  5. Serve: Soak Ras Malai discs in rabri, garnish with chopped nuts.

Jalebi


Ingredients


Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp cornflour (optional)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • A pinch of turmeric or saffron (optional)

Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder

For Frying:

  • Oil or ghee

Recipe


  1. Prepare sugar syrup: Boil sugar and water. Add cardamom and lemon juice. Keep warm.

  2. Prepare batter: Mix flour, cornflour, yogurt, water, and baking powder to a smooth batter. Let ferment 6-8 hours for best results.

  3. Fry Jalebis: Heat oil on medium flame. Pour batter in a spiral shape using a piping bag or bottle. Fry until golden and crisp.

  4. SOak in syrup: Immerse fried jalebis in warm sugar syrup for 1-2 minutes. Remove and serve hot.

Kheer


Ingredients


  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 10-12 cashews, almonds, raisins
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Recipe


  1. Wash & Soak: Rinse basmati rice 2–3 times until water runs clear. Soak in water for 30 minutes, then drain.

  2. Boil Milk: Heat milk in a heavy-bottom pan. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low flame and simmer, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Add Rice: Add the soaked rice to simmering milk. Cook on low flame for 30–35 minutes, stirring often, until rice grains are soft and the mixture thickens.

  4. Sweeten: Add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands. Stir continuously until sugar dissolves completely and kheer reaches a creamy consistency.

  5. Garnish: In a small pan, fry cashews, almonds, and raisins in 1 tsp ghee until golden. Add them to the kheer, mix well. Serve warm or chilled after 1–2 hours of refrigeration.

Besan Ladoo


Ingredients


  • 2 cups gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 3/4 cup ghee
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp chopped dry fruits

Recipe


  1. Roast Flour: Heat ghee in a heavy-bottom pan on low flame. Add besan (gram flour) and roast slowly, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Roast for 20–25 minutes until the mixture turns golden brown and releases a nutty aroma.

  2. Cool: Switch off the flame and let the roasted mixture cool slightly (it should be warm, not hot). Add cardamom powder and mix well.

  3. Mix Sugar: Once lukewarm, add powdered sugar gradually, mixing thoroughly to avoid lumps. The mixture should come together and feel soft but firm enough to shape.

  4. Shape: Take small portions of the mixture in your palm and roll into smooth round ladoos. Press a piece of dry fruit (cashew/almond) on top for garnish.

  5. Store: Allow ladoos to set completely. Store in an airtight container; they remain fresh for up to 2 weeks at room temperature.

Coconut Barfi


Ingredients


  • 2 cups grated fresh coconut
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped pistachios for garnish

Recipe


  1. Cook Mixture: In a heavy-bottom pan, add milk and sugar. Stir on medium flame until sugar dissolves completely and the mixture turns slightly thick (about 5–7 minutes).

  2. Add Coconut: Add the grated fresh coconut and mix well. Cook on low flame, stirring continuously, so the mixture doesn’t stick or burn.

  3. Flavor: Add ghee and cardamom powder. Continue cooking until the mixture thickens, turns glossy, and starts leaving the sides of the pan (10–12 minutes).

  4. Set: Grease a tray or plate with ghee. Transfer the hot mixture, spread it evenly using a spatula, and smooth the surface.

  5. Cut: Allow it to cool for 30–40 minutes at room temperature. Once firm, cut into squares or diamond shapes and garnish with chopped pistachios or almonds.
